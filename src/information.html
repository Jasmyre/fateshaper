Advanced Rock-Paper-Scissors Combat

A comprehensive guide to Fateshaper's mechanics, stats, and strategic depth.

Overview

This game is a next-level spin on the classic rock-paper-scissors. It's not just about picking rock, paper, or scissors—there's an entire RPG-style system working behind the scenes. You'll need to manage stats like strength, precision, crit, speed, fatigue, and momentum, which all affect every move in combat. This makes each battle more strategic, dynamic, and unpredictable.

Strategic Depth

Beyond simple RPS, every decision is influenced by your stats, fatigue, and momentum.

RPG Elements

Manage and upgrade six core stats to customize your combat style and strategy.

Class System

Choose from five unique classes, each with different stat distributions and special abilities.

Game Lore

In a world where even the simplest gesture can change the tide of battle...

In this universe, champions from different classes (such as Warrior, Mage, Rogue, Guardian, and Assassin) clash using the timeless moves of rock, paper, and scissors. Behind each move is a complex interplay of fatigue and momentum—winning builds momentum and makes your strikes more lethal, while overexertion raises fatigue and diminishes your defenses. Choose your moves carefully to outlast your opponent.

The Five Classes

Warrior

Powerful frontline fighters with high health and defense. Warriors excel at absorbing damage while dealing consistent attacks.

Mage

Masters of arcane power with high damage and healing abilities. Mages are fragile but can devastate opponents with powerful attacks.

Rogue

Swift and cunning fighters with balanced stats. Rogues excel at speed and damage, making them versatile in combat situations.

Guardian

Stalwart protectors with exceptional health and defense. Guardians can withstand massive damage and excel in prolonged battles.

Assassin

Masters of precision and speed. Assassins deal devastating damage but are extremely fragile, requiring careful positioning.

Gameplay

Core Mechanics

Rounds

The game is divided into rounds. In each round, you select an action (attack with rock, paper, or scissors, skip your turn, or heal), and your opponent (a robot) picks an action at random.

Outcomes

Traditional rock–paper–scissors rules apply: rock beats scissors, scissors beats paper, and paper beats rock. Ties occur when both players choose the same move.

Attacks & Damage

When one side wins a round, an attack is triggered. The damage you deal is calculated using your current stats combined with situational modifiers like fatigue and momentum.

Strategic Options

Skip

Sometimes, you may choose to skip your turn. This can be strategic when you're too fatigued—skipping can help reduce fatigue but may give your opponent a chance to counterattack.

Heal

You can opt to heal instead of attacking. Healing recovers some health, but it also increases your fatigue, so it must be used judiciously.

Upgrades

After every round, both you and the robot earn upgrade points. These points let you boost your stats (strength, precision, crit, speed, defense, healing) to improve your combat performance.

Core Stats

Strength

Determines the raw power behind your attacks. Higher strength means more potential damage.

Precision

Affects your chance to land an attack successfully. Higher precision means fewer misses.

Crit

Governs your likelihood of scoring a critical hit, which multiplies your damage (typically by 1.5x).

Speed

Helps reduce the damage you take by allowing you to dodge or mitigate attacks. Each point reduces damage by 5%.

Defense

Reduces the amount of damage you receive. Each point in defense reduces damage by 3%.

Healing

Determines how much health you regain when you choose to heal. Higher healing means more health recovered.

Dynamic Modifiers

Fatigue

Builds up as you fight. The higher your fatigue, the less effective your stats become, and the more damage you take. Fatigue reduces your effective strength, precision, and crit.

Stat Reduction = Base Stat × (1 - Fatigue/100)

Momentum

Grows when you win rounds consecutively, boosting your overall attack performance. Momentum increases your effective strength, precision, and crit.

Stat Boost = Base Stat × (1 + Momentum/100)

Critical Hits

When your attack lands a critical hit, it deals 50% more damage. Your crit stat determines the chance of landing a critical hit.

Crit Chance = Effective Crit / 10

Effective Stats

Your actual performance stats are calculated by combining your base stats with fatigue and momentum modifiers.

Effective Stat = Base Stat × (1 + Momentum/100) × (1 - Fatigue/100)

Damage Calculation

The damage dealt in combat is determined through a series of mathematical steps that follow the PEMDAS rule (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction). Here's how it works:

1. Effective Strength (ES)

Adjusts the attacker's base strength by factoring in momentum and fatigue.

ES = Strength × (1 + Momentum/100) × (1 - Fatigue/100)

2. Base Damage (BD)

Originally, BD was determined by multiplying ES with a random variation factor between 0.8 and 1.2. In the latest version, this variation has been removed for consistency.

BD = ES × 1

3. Critical Hit Check

A random roll determines whether a critical hit occurs. If it does, the damage is multiplied by a fixed factor (e.g., 1.5).

If Random(0-100) < Effective Crit/10, then Crit Multiplier = 1.5, else Crit Multiplier = 1

Speed Reduction

The defender's speed reduces incoming damage. Each point in speed cuts the damage by a certain percentage.

Speed Reduction = Defender Speed × 0.05

5. Damage after Speed

The damage after applying the speed reduction.

Damage after Speed = BD × Crit Multiplier × (1 - Speed Reduction)

6. Effective Defense

The defender's defense is reduced based on their fatigue.

ED = Defender Defense × (1 - Defender Fatigue/100)

7. Defense Reduction Factor (DRF)

Calculates the percentage of damage that isn't blocked by the defender's effective defense.

DRF = 1 - (ED × 0.03)

8. Damage after Reduction

The damage after applying the defense reduction.

Damage after Reduction = Damage after Speed × DRF

9. Fatigue Damage Multiplier

The higher the defender's fatigue, the more damage they take.

FDM = 1 + (Defender Fatigue/100 × FATIGUE_DAMAGE_FACTOR)

10. Final Damage

The final damage is the damage after reduction multiplied by the fatigue damage multiplier, rounded down.

Final Damage = Floor(Damage after Reduction × FDM)
